Dr. Norman Borlaug

Dr. Norman Borlaug (1914-2009) was an American agronomist and humanitarian who is often referred to as the "father of the Green Revolution." His work in developing high-yielding, disease-resistant wheat varieties helped alleviate hunger and poverty in many parts of the world, particularly in Mexico, India, and Pakistan. Borlaug's contributions to agriculture earned him the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970.
